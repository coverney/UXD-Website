Design Brief

A bit about the problem we are trying to solve

Problem

What is the problem in brief?

When first entering the world of college applications, it can be very daunting- especially to parents experiencing this wild ride for the first time. Tutors, concelours, financial aid, SATs, ACTs, superscore, subjects tests - these are just a few of the terms that you often hear when a student is applying to college. And this crucial time can be even more challenging to parents of low-income families that cannot afford fancy college counselors or tutors or all the books on college.

Our Solution

How will your project change the status quo? For whom?

Our project strives to connect two user groups, parents with children who left for college (i.e. empty nesters), and parents of highschool juniors and seniors that are just beginning to learn about the application process. We believe that empty nesters have the potential to serve as an invaluable source/ guide to new college-applicant parents. From what are the best SAT prep books/tutors, to how to apply for financial aid, to the benefits of ED versus EA- these parents have experienced it and are usually excited to share the knowledge. A side benefit is that this also provides a hobby to newly empty nester parents that are adjusting to life without their children at home. It's a win-win.

First Draft Design Idea

What is (at least) one naïve design idea (rough sketch)? How does this idea address what you see as the core problem?

Users sign on via app/webpage interface. When signing up, they create a profile in which they indicate: empty nester/ new college applicant, school familiarity/ interest, general area location, and specific needs (i.e. financial aid process, student athletes). The system will then connect users, empty nester to new college applicant, based on similar interests, experiences, student education, and needs. Overall, the concept is to create a “social network experience” to connect both sides.

The Data

What information do you need to collect? How will you collect it? How will you meet your target users, or who can serve as effective proxies for them?

The information we need to gather is:

  • What are the pain points current parents of college applicants feel?
  • What do they not know enough about?
  • How much money do they currently spend on the college application process?
  • How much do they learn from conclours vs doing their own research?
  • What did they wish they knew before beginning the application process (empty nesters)?
  • And more...
We will start our research process by reaching out to our user groups. We plan to delve into our parents’ connections to find both empty nesters and parents that are helping their children apply to college for the first time. Once we identify potential users, we will arrange phone/ Skype calls to ask them the questions we listed above.

Our Team

How will your team work together to accomplish these goals (as well as your individual learning goals)?

Our team will work together to accomplish this project by equally distributing the work and abiding by our team contract. We will also focus on the design thinking aspects of our project and emphasize our individual learning goals, which include:

  • Shy: Wants to develop his skills in front and back-hand programming and gain more proficiency in software development while including user input
  • Siena: Wants to improve design thinking skills, wants to improve confidence with software skills
  • Cassandra: Wants to learn about design thinking and apply software skills to design decisions
  • Sab: Wants to better understand and compare/contrast different types of user interfaces to see how that contributes to the overall product and experience, and how UXD plays into product management
Shy, Siena, and Cassandra will probably be in charge of future software implementations, and Sab will focus on making sure we create the best interface for our users.
